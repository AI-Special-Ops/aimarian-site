How I Actually Use AI at Work

No jargon. No hype. Just what it does for me every day.

Marian Esco .. April 2026

The problem I was solving

I work in business development. That means I talk to a lot of people, research a lot of companies, write a lot of messages, track a lot of conversations, and make a lot of decisions.. every single day.

Before I built this system, my workday looked something like this:

Notes everywhere. Some in a notes app, some in documents, some in my head. If someone asked me "what did you discuss with that person 3 weeks ago" I'd spend 10 minutes searching.

Context lost between sessions. Every time I started working with an AI assistant, I had to re-explain everything. Who I am, what I do, what I'm working on, what happened yesterday. Like starting every conversation from zero.

Repetitive busywork. Updating files in 3 places. Copy-pasting the same information. Manually syncing things that should sync themselves.

I knew AI could help. But not the way most people think about it.

What I actually built

I didn't build a robot. I built a workspace that remembers everything and keeps itself organized.

Here's what it is, in plain terms:

One central knowledge base (an Obsidian vault) where everything lives. Every person I talk to, every decision I make, every project I'm working on.. it's all in one place, linked together.

Multiple capture methods that feed into it. Terminal commands, phone notes, voice recordings from a wearable pendant, desktop editing. All roads lead to the same knowledge base.

A sync pipeline that keeps everything current. One command and the entire system updates.. my local AI reads from the same files I edit.

That's it. That's the whole system. One brain, multiple ways to feed it, and everything stays organized without me having to think about it.

What a typical day looks like

Morning (3 minutes): I open my terminal and type one command. It shows me what changed overnight, what's out of sync, what I need to focus on today, and whether any notes are sitting unsorted. Three minutes and I know exactly where I stand.

During the day: A thought hits me while I'm on a call? I type a quick command and it's captured. I'm on my phone between meetings? I write a note and it syncs to my laptop automatically. I'm wearing my voice recorder pendant and I say something worth keeping? It gets transcribed and filed.

After a call: I update the person's note. The system connects it to every other interaction, decision, and project related to that person. Next time I need context, it's all there. No digging.

End of day (5 minutes): I fill in what got done, what decisions I made, and what carries forward to tomorrow. I sort any notes that landed in my inbox. One sync command, and everything is up to date across the entire system.

The point isn't the technology. The point is that I spend maybe 10 minutes a day on system maintenance.. and in return, I never lose context, I never forget what was discussed, and I never start from scratch.

What actually changed

Before

30+ minutes daily on file management, searching, context rebuilding.

After

Under 10 minutes. Everything captured, linked, searchable.

Before

Starting every AI session from zero.

After

AI reads from the same organized vault. Full context from day one.

Before

Ideas lost between phone, laptop, voice notes, meetings.

After

Four capture methods, one destination. Nothing falls through.

The simple insight

Most people think "using AI" means asking ChatGPT a question and getting an answer. That's useful, but it's like using a calculator for one equation and then forgetting the result.

What I built is different. My AI doesn't just answer questions.. it remembers. It knows what I'm working on, who I've talked to, what decisions I've made, and what my priorities are. Because I set it up to read from the same organized system I use every day.

The AI isn't the point. The organized system is the point. The AI just makes it 10x more useful b/c it can instantly find, connect, and surface information that would take me hours to dig up manually.

Why this matters for anyone who works with information

You don't need to be a programmer to benefit from this kind of setup. The principles work for anyone:

One source of truth. Don't keep the same information in 5 places. Pick one home for your knowledge, and let everything else pull from it.

Capture everything, sort later. The best system is the one you actually use. If capturing a thought takes more than 10 seconds, you won't do it. Make it effortless.

Connect, don't just store. A note that links to nothing is a note you'll never find again. The value isn't in individual notes.. it's in the connections between them.

Let the machine do the repetitive work. Syncing, organizing, searching, summarizing.. these are things computers are great at. Save your energy for the thinking.

The whole thing runs on my laptop. No cloud subscriptions. No monthly fees. Just a notes app, a terminal tool I built, and a local AI that reads from my own files. Total cost: my time to set it up.
